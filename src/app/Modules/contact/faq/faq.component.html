Help / FAQs

1. What is Should Cost Model Mart?

Should Cost Model Mart is a repository of historically costed models accessible to Procurement teams with role based access.

2. What can I do with Model Mart?

Model Mart can be used for several scenarios some of which are:
  • Float requests for new projects and track their completion status
  • Search & download previously costed models
  • Tweak available cost models to simulate new scenarios and download simulated reports
  • Compare & contrast part numbers/models on various parameters like raw material, weight, should cost etc.
  • Check program wise completion reports
  • Check region and BU wise reports
  • Analyze category costs
  • Simulate BOM costs early in the program lifecycle
  • Evaluate design ideas based on comparison of raw material, processes and cost

3. What are the benefits of Model Mart? Why is it required?

Model Mart can help improve the reuse of existing should cost models and help reduce your waiting time for project request from COE team. It also empowers you to simulate various scenarios and take charge of cost analytics to negotiate better and in a timely fashion with the suppliers.

4. What is the Reuse-Refresh-Request approach in Model Mart?

Lead Times


Reuse

  • Reuse previously costed models
  • DIY tweak for changes in raw material and labor rates

Refresh

  • Ask for a refresh of previous models for:
    ✔ Supplier Manufacturing Location change
    ✔ Changes in supplier process layout
    ✔ Overhead cost changes etc.
  • Get existing models quickly refreshed to meet your needs
  • Priority delivery over new requests

Request

  • Request new models if you cannot find a relevant existing model

5. How do I find out the correct model for my use on Model Mart?

You can use search to look for parts by part number, noun names, CAT2/CAT3/CAT4 and even costed location. Then there are several filters on the left pane to filter those out further. Compare feature can be used by selecting up to 4 parts and comparing them on several criteria to find the right or a close model for use. A part # match or a close design match (similar design) make an available model suited for reuse or refresh.

6. When is the Reuse approach the best?

  • Reuse approach is best applied when you have found the same part number or a close design match (similar design) part on your search in model mart. You can then either use the same model or tweak the model to adjust raw material rate and other variables and use them
  • Reuse approach can also be used when you are pressed for time and need directional estimates with a plan to request new models later. In this case you can use a relatively inadequate design match for your estimates

7. When is Refresh approach the best?

Refresh approach is best applied when you have found the same part number or a close design match (similar design) part on your search in model mart. However, you would like the model to be costed for another geographic location, different supplier process and/or would like to have the process overhead cost refreshed.

8. When is Request approach the best?

Request approach works best when the Reuse and Refresh approach don’t seem appropriate.

9. Why do I need to tweak should cost models in Model Mart?

Some of the costed models might be dated so it makes sense to adjust the models to reflect current market conditions before reusing them.

10. What parameters can be tweaked in a should cost model in Model Mart?

You can adjust raw material rates, labor rates (inflation %), SG&A, Profit, Packaging and Freight to reflect current market conditions.

11. Why can’t I tweak the Process Overhead Cost in available should cost models?

Process overhead cost is a complex ensemble of over 15 parameters which needs the experience of a Cost SME from Tech Cost Management COE to address. Additionally, this bucket is relatively inelastic since many significant contributors to this bucket – like machine depreciation costs don’t change significantly over time. However, if you need the OH cost to be updated, please send a Refresh request via model mart to COE team and they will share an updated model. Refresh requests can be sent via model mart very quickly.

12. Can I see my previously downloaded and tweaked models in history?

Yes, your previous activities on model mart will be accessible to you. You can see the previously downloaded models and tweaked simulations after your login. It is WIP.

13. Why don’t I see any models for my categories/filters?

At the Model Mart launch in July 2024, we have uploaded around 3500+ cost and tooling models for your use. We are in the process of uploading around 8000 models by the end of the year. Therefore, some categories/filters might have no/limited models. In that case, please float a request through Request tab for a new model.

14. What should I do if I am not aware of benchmark indicators such as raw material rates, % inflation in labor, SG&A etc.?

You may send a model for Refresh to COE team who will support. We are currently working on guidance benchmark indicators which we will release in due course of time.

15. How can I glean over the manufacturing process used for a component in Model Mart?

Manufacturing process used to arrive at the should cost found in the report that can downloaded from the Download Model button at the end of project details page.

16. Can I update the weight of the component for a model available in Model Mart?

No, the model should be sent for a Refresh to the Tech Cost Management COE. There can be different considerations like yield%, #cavity, process change etc., that needs to be looked into by an SME.

17. Can I update the assumed supply chain for a component from a Tier 1/Tier 2 structure to a single Tier 1 structure?

You can update the SG&A, Profit, Freight, Packaging headers in Tier 2 cost summary to zero which will reflect an integrated supply chain with a single Tier 1 supplier.

18. Can I update the assumed supply chain for a component from a Tier 1 structure to a Tier 1/Tier 2 structure?

You can send the model for a quick Refresh to COE team.

19. Can non-Procurement functions access this application?

At this moment, this application is only applicable to Procurement teams.

20. Can I use the raw material rate and other details provided by my supplier to tweak a model?

It is advisable to corroborate supplier provided rates with independent research before using them to tweak models.

21. When I update a cost model, does it overwrite the data/model available in database?

No, user update to cost models does not overwrite should cost model data. User updates are calculated real-time and need to be downloaded for future use. The user history section will keep downloaded and tweaked reports for a limited period for future use. It is WIP.

22. Can I download the tweaked model report after my updates?

Yes, the simulated/tweaked cost model can be downloaded by clicking the Download Report button at top right on the Update Model page.

23. How are forex fluctuations reflected in model updates?

Cost models executed for non-US regions are automatically adjusted for changes in forex before showing the new labor cost. Therefore, at times, even with an actual inflationary increase in labor the labor cost value might come down if the non-US currency has depreciated more than the inflation.

24. Why don’t I see Supplier Quote field not populated for some cost models?

We rely on Procurement teams to share back with us the supplier quotes and agreed price with the supplier. The field remains blank wherever these details are not shared with us.
